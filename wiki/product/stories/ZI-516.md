## Open Issues

## User Story

As an **Australian industrial-coatings merchant shipping dangerous goods on TNT AU — where TNT quotes "1–2 business days" at checkout but my warehouse cannot always pack and dispatch dangerous goods that fast**, I want **a per-carrier "handling days" buffer that I configure once and that pads every TNT AU delivery estimate the buyer sees at checkout**, so that **my checkout stops promising delivery dates my fulfillment team cannot honour, my customers stop emailing me asking where their order is on day 3, and I don't lose them to competitors who set honest expectations from the cart**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] TNT AU carrier in Shopify MCSL exposes an "Add Buffer Days to Estimated Delivery" setting in Carrier Settings → Other Details
- [ ] The configured buffer is added to TNT AU's live-rate delivery estimate before it's surfaced at Shopify checkout (so a "1–2 business day" quote with a 2-day buffer becomes "3–4 business days")
- [ ] Sydney Industrial Coatings (#379692, byv77w-th.myshopify.com) is informed once the feature ships and confirms the buffer applies on their actual DG orders
- [ ] The buffer-days mechanism is implemented as a shared per-carrier capability — not a TNT-AU-special-case — so the next carrier ticket asking for buffer days (e.g. ZI-515 FedEx REST) ships from the same mechanism
- [ ] Buffer setting persists across plugin upgrades and is documented in the TNT AU integration help text so future merchants find it without raising a ticket

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: TNT AU buffer days lengthens the customer-visible delivery estimate

- **Given** a Sydney Industrial Coatings order being rated against TNT AU at Shopify checkout, where TNT's API returns "Estimated delivery: 1–2 business days"
- **And** the merchant has configured "Add Buffer Days to Estimated Delivery" = 2 days for the TNT AU carrier
- **When** the buyer sees the rate at checkout
- **Then** the displayed estimated delivery is "3–4 business days" (TNT's 1–2 + the merchant's 2-day handling buffer)
- **And** the rate amount itself is unchanged — only the date/range string is shifted

### Scenario: Dangerous-goods order respects the buffer setting

- **Given** a TNT AU order containing one or more DG-flagged products (which require additional handling/quarantine time at the merchant's end)
- **When** the merchant generates labels in bulk for those orders
- **Then** the buffer that was visible at checkout matches the actual handling window the warehouse plans for
- **And** the buffer is not silently bypassed for DG / multi-package / split-shipment scenarios

### Scenario: Prevention — buffer-days is a shared per-carrier capability, not yet-another-special-case

- **Given** the next carrier integration (or the next FedEx REST account, c.f. ZI-515) needs the same buffer behaviour
- **When** engineering adds the carrier
- **Then** "Add Buffer Days to Estimated Delivery" is available from day one on that carrier's Other Details panel
- **And** the implementation reuses one shared module — not copy/pasted per-carrier code — so a fix or limit change applies everywhere at once
