## User Story

As a **Shopify store manager who frequently updates product names and adds/removes variants as my catalog evolves, and who now has dozens of duplicate entries cluttering my StorePep product list because every edit creates a new entry instead of updating the existing one**,
I want **product changes in Shopify (name updates, variant additions, variant removals) to update the existing StorePep entry in place rather than creating a duplicate**,
so that **my product list reflects reality — one entry per product, not three versions of the same item with different names — and my team can trust the catalog when selecting products for label generation**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Renaming a product in Shopify updates the existing StorePep entry (no duplicate created)
- [ ] Adding or removing variants on a product updates the existing entry's variant list in place
- [ ] Product matching uses Shopify product ID (not product name) so renames don't create new entries
- [ ] Existing duplicate entries from past syncs can be identified and merged via a cleanup utility or manual action
- [ ] David B.'s store shows exactly one entry per Shopify product after the fix ships

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Product rename updates in place
- **Given** "Widget A" exists in StorePep with Shopify product ID 12345
- **When** the merchant renames it to "Widget A Pro" in Shopify
- **Then** StorePep entry 12345 updates to "Widget A Pro"
- **And** no duplicate "Widget A" entry remains

### Scenario: Variant added without duplication
- **Given** "Widget A Pro" has 3 variants (Red, Blue, Black) in StorePep
- **When** the merchant adds a "Green" variant in Shopify
- **Then** StorePep entry shows 4 variants (Red, Blue, Black, Green) on the same product
- **And** no second "Widget A Pro" entry is created

### Scenario: Historical duplicates identifiable
- **Given** StorePep contains "Widget A" and "Widget A Pro" as separate entries (both mapping to Shopify ID 12345)
- **When** a product sync runs after the fix
- **Then** the system detects the duplicate and either merges automatically or flags for merchant review
- **And** subsequent syncs prevent new duplicates from forming
