# Ticket #383757 — FedEx ZPL format unavailable and commercial invoice currency locked to store base currency

- **Customer**: Thomas / LOUIS XVI Team (corsin.jost@louisxviwatches.com) — Switzerland store
- **Product**: Shopify Multi Carrier Shipping Label
- **Duration**: 2026-04-13 to 2026-04-13 (1 day, ongoing)

## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: FedEx Label Format Issue (2026-04-13 morning)
- Customer reported FedEx labels only available as PDF/Print, both rendering at half-page with excessive white space; unusable for thermal printer workflow
- ZPL format needed for thermal printer but unavailable for FedEx (works correctly for UPS)
- Support (Touseef) suggested PNG image type as workaround
- Customer confirmed PNG format resolved the label printing issue (Comment #4)

### Phase 2: Invoice Currency Mismatch Escalation (2026-04-13 afternoon)
- After label fix, customer raised new issue: commercial invoices and shipping documents display EUR (store base currency) regardless of order destination
- Store accepts payments in 10+ currencies; test order placed from US still showed EUR instead of USD on invoice
- Customer needs documents to reflect recipient's local currency for operational and compliance reasons
- Support requested collaborator access and sample orders to investigate (Comment #5)
- Customer granted collaborator access and shared test order with screenshots (Comment #6)
- Awaiting PluginHive investigation

## Open Issues

1. **Commercial invoice displays store base currency instead of order currency** — International orders show EUR on invoices and shipping documents regardless of the destination country currency. Customer placed a test order from the US and the invoice still showed EUR instead of USD. Store accepts 10+ currencies and needs documents to reflect the customer's payment currency for operational and compliance reasons. Blocked: customer (awaiting PluginHive investigation via collaborator access; may require product change if currency conversion is not currently supported). Severity: high (compliance impact for international shipping business). Area: international. (Comment #6)

2. **FedEx ZPL label format not available** — ZPL format is available for UPS but missing for FedEx in label configuration settings. PNG workaround resolved the immediate printing issue, but the underlying gap in ZPL support for FedEx remains unaddressed. Blocked: customer (workaround in place, root cause not fixed). Severity: low (workaround exists via PNG). Area: carrier-config. (Comment #1)

## Resolved Issues

1. **FedEx label rendering at half-page size** — Resolved by switching image type from PDF/Print to PNG, which prints correctly on thermal printers. (Comment #4)

- **Business type**: Luxury watch e-commerce, international shipping
- **Shipping volume**: 316 orders total, 2 labels generated in April 2026

## User Story

As a **Switzerland-based luxury watch retailer shipping internationally in 10+ currencies on a $19 plan**,
I want **commercial invoices and shipping documents to reflect the currency the buyer actually paid in rather than my store's base EUR**,
so that **my customs documentation is compliant with destination-country import requirements and my customers do not see a confusing currency mismatch that undermines trust in a high-value purchase**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Commercial invoices display the order's presentment currency (the currency the customer paid in), not the store's base currency
- [ ] A multi-currency Shopify store with EUR base currency generates a USD invoice for a US-based order paid in USD
- [ ] All carrier document types (commercial invoice, proforma invoice, shipping documents) respect the order presentment currency consistently
- [ ] Existing single-currency stores are unaffected — invoices continue to use the store's only currency
- [ ] The fix is verified against LOUIS XVI's store (collaborator access already granted) with their test order scenario

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Invoice currency matches order presentment currency
- **Given** a Shopify store with base currency EUR and multi-currency enabled (10+ currencies)
- **When** a customer places an order from the US and pays in USD, and the merchant generates a FedEx commercial invoice
- **Then** the commercial invoice displays all amounts in USD, matching the order's presentment currency

### Scenario: Invoice currency correct across all supported carriers
- **Given** a multi-currency store generating international shipping documents
- **When** the merchant generates a commercial invoice via any supported carrier (FedEx, UPS, DHL, etc.)
- **Then** the invoice currency matches the order's presentment currency regardless of carrier

### Scenario: Single-currency stores remain unaffected
- **Given** a Shopify store with only one currency configured (no multi-currency)
- **When** the merchant generates a commercial invoice for any order
- **Then** the invoice displays the store's single currency as it does today, with no behavioral change
