## User Story

As a **Shopify merchant comparing MCSL plans on the storefront before I install — sometimes I have a 5k SKU catalogue, sometimes a 78k one, and the right plan for my catalogue size is not the same as the right plan for my shipment volume**,
I want **MCSL's Shopify pricing page and in-app plan picker to spell out the catalogue-size limit per tier ("Basic supports up to N products") with the same prominence as the shipment-volume limit, and for the app to refuse to silently put me on a tier that won't support my actual catalogue**,
so that **I don't reinstall on the cheapest plan during my eval (like Brad did at 78k products on $9 Basic), waste support time hitting limits PluginHive already knows I'd hit, or churn on day 30 when bulk operations start failing — and PluginHive captures the right ARPU upfront from large-catalogue customers instead of relying on a support nudge to upsell**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Each MCSL plan tier (Basic, Popular, Enterprise, any others) has an explicit product-count limit visible on the Shopify App Store pricing page and inside MCSL's in-app plan picker — phrased as "Up to N products / variants in your Shopify catalogue", not buried in fine print
- [ ] Limits are derived from observable scaling thresholds (CSV export viability, sync performance, etc.) and documented internally so support can defend them — not arbitrary marketing numbers
- [ ] When a merchant installs MCSL and their Shopify catalogue size already exceeds the chosen tier's product limit, the app surfaces a one-time interstitial recommending the appropriate tier before any sync runs, and links to the upgrade flow — so merchants like Brad don't silently land on Basic with 78k products
- [ ] Plan downgrade is blocked (or warned with explicit override) when the merchant's current catalogue size exceeds the destination tier's limit, so an Enterprise customer downgrading to Popular can't end up in a half-working state
- [ ] The customers in #389897 (ez90xj-ps, 78k products) and #387563 (Anandhaas, $19-cap hit) are reachable with the new policy — Brad sees the right tier recommended at reinstall, Anandhaas sees catalogue-size guidance alongside the label-count guidance

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Brad reinstalls MCSL on a 78k-product store and is steered to Enterprise before sync
- **Given** a Shopify merchant with 78,000+ products in their Shopify catalogue installs MCSL and lands on the plan-picker screen
- **When** they're about to select the $9 Basic plan
- **Then** MCSL shows a non-dismissible recommendation that Basic's product-count limit is far below their catalogue, recommends the Enterprise tier with the specific catalogue-size limit shown, and provides a one-click jump to the Enterprise subscription rather than letting them complete the Basic selection silently

### Scenario: Anandhaas-style growth merchant sees both the label cap AND the catalogue cap upfront
- **Given** a merchant on the $19 starter plan whose Shopify catalogue is under the Basic catalogue limit but whose monthly label generation is approaching the Basic label cap
- **When** they view their in-app plan picker (or the Shopify App Store pricing page) before MCSL sends them a "you're near your label limit" nudge
- **Then** the pricing page clearly shows both axes — label volume per month AND catalogue product count — for each tier, so the merchant can self-pick the right next tier instead of waiting for a support outreach

### Scenario: Limits are enforced consistently between Shopify App Store and in-app
- **Given** a merchant who reads the Shopify App Store pricing page and concludes that Basic supports up to N products
- **When** they install MCSL and reach the in-app plan picker
- **Then** the in-app picker shows the same N as the App Store, the limit is enforced by the same backend rule (no drift between marketing copy and runtime check), and a single change to the limit propagates to both surfaces without engineering touching the storefront text
