## User Story

As a **returning merchant with a 78k-product Shopify catalog who ships internationally and needs per-product customs descriptions (HS codes, country of origin, declared values) on every shipment**,
I want **clear plan-tier boundaries that tell me upfront how many products each plan supports, and a CSV export that actually works for catalogs at my scale**,
so that **I can stop guessing which plan to buy and stop waiting weeks for L3 manual exports just to update customs fields — every day without bulk updates means either hand-editing thousands of products or shipping with missing customs data and risking border holds**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Each pricing plan (Basic, Popular, Premium, Enterprise) defines and enforces a maximum synced-product count, visible on the Shopify app listing and in-app plan-selection screen
- [ ] CSV product export completes successfully for catalogs with 78k+ products (with 1-3 variants each), delivering a downloadable file within a reasonable time window and surfacing a completion notification
- [ ] When a merchant on a plan that does not support their catalog size attempts a CSV export, the app shows a clear message explaining the limit and which plan to upgrade to — not a silent failure
- [ ] Brad's specific store (ez90xj-ps.myshopify.com, 78k+ products) can export and re-import a product CSV with customs descriptions after upgrading to the appropriate plan tier
- [ ] The Shopify app listing pricing page reflects the updated product-count limits per tier before the feature ships to production

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Large-catalog CSV export succeeds on a supported plan
- **Given** a merchant on the Enterprise plan with 78,000+ synced products (1-3 variants each)
- **When** they initiate a product CSV export (full catalog or filtered selection)
- **Then** the export completes, a notification is delivered, and the CSV file is available for download containing all requested products with their variant-level customs fields

### Scenario: Plan-tier product limit is enforced with clear messaging
- **Given** a merchant on the Basic ($9) plan whose Shopify store has 78k+ products synced
- **When** product sync runs or they attempt a CSV export exceeding the Basic plan's product limit
- **Then** the app displays a message stating the current plan supports up to N products, their store has M products, and recommends the specific plan tier that covers their catalog size

### Scenario: Silent export failure is eliminated
- **Given** any merchant on any plan who initiates a product CSV export
- **When** the export cannot complete (due to plan limits, timeout, server error, or any other reason)
- **Then** the app delivers an explicit failure notification within 5 minutes explaining why the export did not complete and what action to take, instead of failing silently with no feedback
