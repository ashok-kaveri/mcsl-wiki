## User Story

As a **newly-installed MCSL merchant whose very first email from PluginHive is an auto-welcome that looks like I sent it to myself**,
I want **that first-touch email to come from a recognisable human at PluginHive (the CSM or onboarding owner for my store) with a real reply-to that routes back into support — not a Zendesk-shaped self-loop that my mail server is going to file as spam**,
so that **I actually see the onboarding offer in my inbox on day one (not buried under "promotions" three days later), I know who to reply to when I'm ready to schedule the call, and I don't churn out before PluginHive's CS team ever gets a chance to reach me**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] The MCSL install welcome email's From: header is a real CSM/onboarding-owner identity at the @pluginhive.com domain (e.g. "Touseef from PluginHive <touseef@pluginhive.com>"), not the installing merchant's own address echoed back at them
- [ ] The Reply-To header is `support@pluginhive.com` (or `support@pluginhive.zendesk.com`) so any merchant reply opens or appends to a Zendesk ticket automatically
- [ ] The email body addresses the merchant by their actual Shopify store name (not generic "Hello"), and includes the installing merchant's store URL so the recipient can verify it's about them specifically
- [ ] After the change, ≥95% of welcome emails sent in a 7-day sample window pass SPF, DKIM, and DMARC alignment checks against the pluginhive.com domain (so they don't get spam-foldered for sender-mismatch reasons)
- [ ] The MCSL uninstall win-back email follows the same sender + reply-to + personalization rules — install and uninstall flows fix together, not one at a time

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: A fresh MCSL install produces a human-from welcome email that lands in inbox
- **Given** a new Shopify merchant installs MCSL and Zendesk emits the auto-welcome
- **When** the email arrives at the merchant's inbox
- **Then** the From: header shows a PluginHive CSM identity (not the merchant's own address), the Reply-To routes back to PluginHive support, the body greets the merchant by store name, and the email passes the merchant mailbox's SPF/DKIM/DMARC checks (no warning banner, no spam folder placement on standard Gmail/Outlook/Apple Mail)

### Scenario: Merchant reply to the welcome email creates or appends a support ticket cleanly
- **Given** the new merchant replies to the welcome email asking a setup question
- **When** the reply leaves their mail client
- **Then** it lands in PluginHive's Zendesk queue against the same ticket thread (not as a brand-new ticket each time), the original CSM/onboarding-owner is the assignee or on-CC, and no message is lost to a self-loop where the reply goes back to the merchant

### Scenario: Uninstall win-back email gets the same treatment, not just install
- **Given** an existing MCSL merchant uninstalls the app and Zendesk triggers the win-back email
- **When** the uninstall email is delivered to the merchant
- **Then** it uses the same human From: identity and Reply-To convention as the install email, addresses the merchant by their store name, and any reply routes into support — so the merchant has a real human to talk to about why they uninstalled (not an apparent self-email that gets ignored)
