# Ticket #395154 — Add Residential/Commercial Surcharge Support for TNT Australia Rates

- **Customer**: Jason Gendrolius (nautcases.com)
- **Product**: Shopify Multi Carrier Shipping Label
- **Duration**: 2026-06-15 to 2026-06-18 (4 days, ongoing)

## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: Customer Reports Inaccurate TNT Quoting (2026-06-15)
- Customer reported checkout rates from TNT are inaccurate because the app does not present a residential vs. commercial address selector
- TNT Australia (Nick Evans, Customer Solutions Specialist) confirmed via email that residential surcharge support exists in their API but PluginHive has not implemented it
- TNT explicitly directed customer to raise with PluginHive and offered to work through API requirements

### Phase 2: Internal Triage and TNT Contact Established (2026-06-15 to 2026-06-16)
- Support (Priyanka) requested customer CC the TNT contact; customer provided Nick Evans' email (nicholas.evans@fedex.com)
- Priyanka sent API logs to Nick Evans and requested documentation on residential surcharge parameters
- Nick responded with implementation details: add `<residential>RSD</residential>` (delivery) and `<residential>RSP</residential>` (pickup/collection) tags to the rate request; confirmed one line of code per address, no address validation required; recommended a toggle checkbox for customers (Comment #16)

### Phase 3: L3 Escalation and Dev Commitment (2026-06-17)
- L3 escalation raised to Slack with full TNT API requirements and attached documentation
- Engineering (Veena) confirmed the app will be enhanced to include residential surcharge for TNT Australia rates; ETA committed for on or before 7th July (Comment #32)
- Support informed both Nick Evans and Jason of the upcoming enhancement

### Phase 4: Unrelated Dimension Issue Discovered and Resolved (2026-06-17)
- Customer raised a new complaint: all international shipping rates failing (Chile, UK, US)
- Support diagnosed via call: total girth exceeded carrier limit of 140 inches due to customer's recent product dimension update
- Customer acknowledged and agreed to revert dimensions; confirmed fix via "Thanks" reply

## Open Issues

1. **TNT Australia Residential Surcharge Not Supported in Rate Requests** — The MCSL app does not pass residential/commercial address type flags in TNT Australia rate API requests, causing residential deliveries to be underquoted at checkout. TNT has confirmed the API supports `<residential>RSD</residential>` and `<residential>RSP</residential>` tags and provided documentation. Engineering has committed to implement this with ETA on or before 7th July 2026. Blocked: customer (cannot get accurate rates until shipped); engineering (development in progress). Severity: confirmed revenue impact — customer is receiving incorrect checkout quotes and has had to process international orders manually. Area: rate-shopping. (Comment #16, #19, #20, #32)

## Resolved Issues

1. **International Rates Failing Due to Exceeded Girth Limit** — All international carrier rates (Chile, UK, US) were returning errors. Diagnosed on a support call (Comment #28): total girth exceeded the carrier's 140-inch limit because the customer had recently updated product dimensions. Customer agreed to revert the dimension changes. Resolved on 2026-06-17. (Comment #28, #29)

## User Story

As a **small Australian retailer shipping protective cases domestically and internationally via TNT Australia on a $19/month plan**,
I want **checkout rates that account for whether deliveries go to residential versus commercial addresses**,
so that **I stop underquoting customers on residential deliveries, eating the surcharge difference on every order, and being forced to manually process international shipments because I cannot trust the automated rates**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] TNT Australia rate requests include the `<residential>RSD</residential>` tag for residential delivery addresses
- [ ] TNT Australia rate requests include the `<residential>RSP</residential>` tag for residential pickup/collection addresses
- [ ] A merchant-facing toggle or address-type selector allows marking addresses as residential or commercial before rate calculation
- [ ] Jason Gendrolius (nautcases.com, ticket #395154) receives accurate TNT checkout rates for residential deliveries without manual intervention
- [ ] Existing TNT Australia merchants who do not configure the residential flag continue to receive rates without regression (backward-compatible default behavior)

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Residential delivery surcharge included in TNT rate
- **Given** a TNT Australia carrier is configured and the destination address is marked as residential
- **When** the app sends a rate request to the TNT Australia API
- **Then** the request payload includes `<residential>RSD</residential>` and the returned rate reflects the residential delivery surcharge

### Scenario: Commercial address does not trigger residential surcharge
- **Given** a TNT Australia carrier is configured and the destination address is marked as commercial (or left as default)
- **When** the app sends a rate request to the TNT Australia API
- **Then** the request payload does not include the residential surcharge tag and the returned rate reflects standard commercial pricing

### Scenario: Backward compatibility for merchants without residential configuration
- **Given** an existing TNT Australia merchant has not configured any residential/commercial address setting
- **When** checkout rate requests are triggered for their store
- **Then** rates are returned using current default behavior with no errors or regressions
