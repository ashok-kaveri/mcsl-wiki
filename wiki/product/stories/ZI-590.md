# Ticket #384139 — TNT Australia Rates Fail After Pickup Cutoff Time; Automatic Next-Day Slot Selection Not Implemented

- **Customer**: Sahas Pokhrel, Project Manager at WP Creative (csid.com.au)
- **Product**: Shopify Multi Carrier Shipping Label
- **Duration**: 2026-04-15 to 2026-06-18 (65 days, ongoing)

## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: Initial Rate Failure — Incorrect Pickup Time (2026-04-15)
- Customer reported rates failing at checkout after store closing time, blocking 24/7 order placement.
- Support identified that the TNT Australia pickup time in plugin settings had been set to 2 PM, which exceeded the regional depot cutoff.
- Support manually corrected it to 1 PM; rates resumed. Customer asked not to CC their client (ezabelle@csid.com.au) on tickets.

### Phase 2: Recurring Failure — Same Root Cause Resurfaces (2026-04-17)
- Rates failed again despite pickup time remaining at 1 PM. Support toggled the setting between 1 PM, 2 PM, 3 PM — 2 PM and 3 PM still failed; 1 PM worked again.
- Customer escalated urgency, requested JSON responses and direct developer access.
- Internal Slack thread opened (C02E15X8X0C). Technical explanation shared: plugin passes current-day date before cutoff, and next working day after cutoff. When TNT blocks slots due to capacity constraints, requests with any time fail intermittently.
- Engineering acknowledged the issue; ticket tagged dev_needed.

### Phase 3: Fallback Rate Feature Deployed (2026-05-26 to 2026-06-04)
- Engineering introduced a Fallback Rate option at the service level for TNT Australia, allowing a fixed rate to display when live rates cannot be fetched.
- Support communicated to customer (Comment #24): fallback rates are configurable under Plugin Settings > Shipping Rates > Carrier Services > TNT Australia > Enable Fallback Rates.
- Engineering confirmed automatic next-available-slot scheduling is still in development (ETA on or before 2026-07-07, per Comment #25).
- A second customer reported the same issue (Ticket #393054), confirming this is not isolated.

## User Story

As a **digital agency project manager operating an Australian client's Shopify store on a $29/month plan with TNT Australia as a primary carrier**,
I want **the plugin to automatically advance rate requests to the next available TNT pickup slot when the current day's collection capacity is exhausted**,
so that **my client's customers aren't blocked from completing checkout after business hours, which erodes buyer confidence and forces me to explain recurring outages to a client who's already frustrated with 65 days of intermittent failures**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] When TNT Australia returns a slot-unavailable or capacity-blocked response for the current pickup day, the plugin automatically retries with the next available business day
- [ ] Live TNT rates are displayed at checkout 24/7 without requiring fallback rate configuration as a workaround
- [ ] The csid.com.au store (Account ID fa26708b-960c-48a0-a22f-29c03f3c1428) receives live TNT rates after depot cutoff time without manual intervention
- [ ] The auto-advance logic handles weekends and public holidays (Australian calendar) by skipping to the next valid business day
- [ ] Existing fallback rate configuration is preserved and continues to function as a safety net alongside the auto-advance feature

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: TNT slot exhausted after depot cutoff — auto-advance to next business day
- **Given** a Shopify store with TNT Australia configured and the current time is past the regional depot pickup cutoff (e.g., 1 PM AEST)
- **When** a customer requests shipping rates at checkout and TNT returns a slot-unavailable response for today
- **Then** the plugin automatically retries the rate request using the next available business day and returns live TNT rates to the checkout

### Scenario: TNT slot blocked mid-week due to capacity constraints
- **Given** a Shopify store with TNT Australia configured and TNT has blocked all remaining collection slots for the current day due to operational capacity
- **When** a customer requests shipping rates at checkout on a weekday before depot cutoff
- **Then** the plugin detects the capacity block, advances to the next day with available slots, and returns live rates without falling back to a fixed rate

### Scenario: Weekend/holiday boundary — skip non-business days
- **Given** a Shopify store with TNT Australia configured and the current day is Friday after cutoff (or a public holiday)
- **When** a customer requests shipping rates at checkout
- **Then** the plugin advances the pickup date to the next available business day (skipping Saturday, Sunday, and any Australian public holidays) and returns live TNT rates
