## User Story

As a **Shopify merchant who ships heavy or palletized freight (150+ lbs) and currently manages TForce LTL shipments outside the app — manually entering addresses on TForce's portal and copy-pasting tracking numbers back into Shopify — consolidated from 3 separate customer requests (#270058, #263693, #274726)**,
I want **TForce Freight available as a carrier in MCSL with rate quoting, Bill of Lading generation, and tracking for LTL shipments**,
so that **I manage all my shipping in one app instead of context-switching to a separate portal for every heavy shipment, and my team stops losing 15 minutes per freight order on manual data entry that the app should handle**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] TForce Freight appears in carrier dropdown after merchant connects their TForce account
- [ ] Rate quoting returns TForce LTL quotes with transit time and freight class for eligible shipments
- [ ] Bill of Lading (BOL) generation produces valid TForce shipping documents with PRO number
- [ ] Tracking status syncs from TForce API into MCSL order timeline
- [ ] All 3 requesting customers (#270058, #263693, #274726) are notified when the integration ships

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Merchant connects TForce account
- **Given** a Shopify merchant with TForce Freight account credentials
- **When** they enter credentials in MCSL carrier settings
- **Then** TForce appears as available carrier for orders exceeding parcel weight thresholds
- **And** a test API call confirms connectivity before saving

### Scenario: LTL freight label generation
- **Given** an order with a 200 lb palletized shipment from Chicago to Los Angeles
- **When** the merchant selects TForce and generates shipping documents
- **Then** a valid BOL PDF is returned with PRO number and freight class
- **And** tracking syncs to Shopify order timeline as TForce scans the freight

### Scenario: Rate comparison includes LTL
- **Given** a merchant with TForce and FedEx Freight both enabled
- **When** rate shopping runs for a 300 lb shipment
- **Then** both carriers return LTL quotes with transit time
- **And** TForce rates reflect the merchant's negotiated contract pricing
