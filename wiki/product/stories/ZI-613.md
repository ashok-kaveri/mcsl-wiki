# Ticket #393842 — Add Buffer Days to USPS Estimated Delivery at Checkout

- **Customer**: Mark Beyerl (retailrems.com)
- **Product**: Shopify Multi Carrier Shipping Label
- **Duration**: 2026-06-05 to 2026-06-18 (14 days, ongoing)

## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: Customer Reports Inaccurate USPS Delivery Estimates (2026-06-05)
- Mark contacted live chat reporting USPS estimated delivery times shown at checkout are inaccurate — showing 1–2 days to Oklahoma from Charlotte NC, which is not realistic
- Support (Keerthi) confirmed the app currently has no option to add buffer/handling days for USPS
- Customer shared a screenshot showing the problematic checkout estimate
- Customer noted the WooCommerce version of the app already has a "Processing Time" / handling days feature in General Settings and explicitly requested parity in the Shopify app
- Customer context clarified: ships urgent rescue kits (broken hydraulic filter kits) where customers are time-sensitive; inaccurate estimates cause customers to choose USPS (cheaper but slower) over better options, defeating the purpose of rate shopping

### Phase 2: Feature Request Escalated to Product Team (2026-06-05 to 2026-06-06)
- L3 internal note raised: "Feature Request — USPS: Add Buffer days to Estimated Delivery Days"
- Internal decision made to enhance the app to add buffer days for USPS; slotted for Release 384 (Comment #8)
- Customer notified via email that the feature is being built and updates will follow (Comment #9)

### Phase 3: ETA Set (2026-06-18)
- Internal note added: ETA on or before 7th July (Comment #10)
- Ticket remains open pending delivery and customer confirmation

## User Story

As a **specialty rescue-kit supplier on the $29/month plan shipping time-critical parts via FedEx, UPS, and USPS**,
I want **to add handling/buffer days to USPS estimated delivery dates so checkout shows realistic arrival windows that account for fulfillment and transit variability**,
so that **my customers stop choosing the cheapest-but-slowest USPS option based on artificially optimistic ETAs, which leads to late deliveries, support calls, and lost trust in a product line where every hour matters**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Merchant can configure buffer days (0–14) for USPS estimated delivery dates in app settings
- [ ] Buffer days are added to the USPS delivery estimate displayed at Shopify checkout
- [ ] retailrems.com (UUID c4b7a357-4592-46b4-9e6b-357ab9dcfbb6) sees corrected USPS estimates on their Charlotte NC → Oklahoma route after enabling buffer days
- [ ] Buffer days configuration does not affect FedEx or UPS delivery estimates unless separately configured
- [ ] Feature achieves parity with the existing WooCommerce app "Processing Time" / handling days functionality in General Settings

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Merchant adds buffer days for USPS
- **Given** a merchant has USPS configured and sets buffer days to 3 in app settings
- **When** a customer at checkout requests USPS rates for a shipment from Charlotte NC to Oklahoma
- **Then** the estimated delivery date displayed adds 3 days to the carrier-returned estimate (e.g., carrier says 2 days → checkout shows 5 days)

### Scenario: Buffer days apply per-carrier without cross-contamination
- **Given** a merchant has USPS buffer set to 3 and FedEx buffer set to 0 (or unconfigured)
- **When** a customer at checkout sees both USPS and FedEx rate options
- **Then** USPS estimates include the 3-day buffer and FedEx estimates remain unmodified

### Scenario: Shopify business-day conversion does not double-compress buffered estimates
- **Given** the app adds buffer days to USPS delivery estimates before Shopify processes them
- **When** Shopify converts the estimate to business days (excluding weekends)
- **Then** the final displayed estimate still reflects a realistic delivery window that accounts for both the buffer and the business-day conversion
