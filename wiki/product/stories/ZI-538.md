## User Story

As an **MCSL merchant shipping with Australia Post eParcel or MPB who would otherwise be generating labels with `null` recipient phone numbers right up until 4 August 2026 when AusPost starts rejecting them**,
I want **MCSL to validate that recipient phone (or email) is present before it ever sends an AusPost label request, warn me clearly at label-generation time which orders are missing the field, and let me fix them in-app instead of discovering the rejection in the AusPost portal**,
so that **on the morning of 4 August I am not staring at a queue of rejected labels with no idea which orders are bad, my fulfilment team isn't manually editing Shopify orders one by one under deadline pressure, and AusPost doesn't pull our integration certification because of a compliance miss that PluginHive had three months of warning to fix**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] When an MCSL order destined for an Australia Post eParcel or MPB shipment lacks a recipient phone (and lacks a recipient email as the alternative AusPost accepts), MCSL no longer passes `null` to AusPost — it surfaces a clear pre-flight warning on the order grid before label generation is attempted
- [ ] At label generation time, if the warning has not been resolved, MCSL blocks the AusPost label request with a specific actionable error ("Order #X: recipient phone or email required for AusPost eParcel/MPB — fix in Shopify or enter in MCSL") rather than letting AusPost reject the request
- [ ] A bulk view shows the merchant ALL of their currently-unfulfilled orders that would fail the new AusPost requirement, so they can fix the data ahead of the 4 Aug 2026 cutover instead of discovering it order-by-order
- [ ] MCSL allows the merchant to enter a fallback recipient phone or email in MCSL itself when Shopify doesn't carry one (e.g. for legacy orders), without forcing them to round-trip through Shopify Admin
- [ ] The change ships and is verified working in production on MCSL well before 2026-08-04, so the change has a buffer window for merchant communications and edge-case discovery

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: MCSL merchant sees AusPost-bound orders without recipient phone before label day
- **Given** an MCSL merchant has 12 unfulfilled Shopify orders destined for AusPost shipping, of which 3 came in from a checkout flow that didn't capture a phone number
- **When** the merchant opens the All Orders grid or the AusPost-specific filtered view in MCSL
- **Then** the 3 affected orders display a clear warning indicator and a specific "recipient phone/email required by AusPost from 2026-08-04" tooltip, so the merchant can prioritise fixing those orders before label day

### Scenario: Attempt to ship an MCSL AusPost label with null phone is blocked at MCSL — not at AusPost
- **Given** an MCSL merchant tries to generate an AusPost eParcel or MPB label for an order where recipient phone AND recipient email are both empty
- **When** they click "Generate Label" on that order (post-2026-08-04)
- **Then** MCSL refuses the request before sending it to AusPost, shows a specific in-app error telling them which field is missing on which order, and provides a one-click action to enter the missing detail in MCSL — they never see a generic AusPost rejection downstream

### Scenario: Backfill flow for the day-zero buffer of orders
- **Given** a fleet of MCSL merchants on the day 2026-08-04 goes live with the AusPost change, where some orders predate the requirement and lack phone data
- **When** any merchant opens MCSL's bulk-edit view for pending AusPost orders
- **Then** they can paste/import a CSV of `order_id, recipient_phone, recipient_email`, or edit individual rows in-app, and the entries propagate to MCSL's AusPost payload — they do NOT have to update Shopify just to get an MCSL label out the door
