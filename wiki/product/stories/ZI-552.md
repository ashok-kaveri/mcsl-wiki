## Ticket Summary

<!-- Source: wiki/zendesk/summaries/390467.md -->

- **Customer**: Unidentified end-recipient (their package late) — store could not be located by support; the ticket was triggered because a merchant tracking-notification email was sent FROM a PluginHive email address, so the recipient's angry reply landed in PluginHive's queue rather than the merchant's
- **Product**: Tag is `shopify` but no specific app tag; lives in `other_platforms`; product-scope item affecting any PluginHive Shopify/notification app where merchants haven't configured their own SMTP (this card scopes MCSL)
- **Duration**: Single-day filing (2026-05-18)

### Timeline & Key Phases

#### Phase 1: End-customer complaint lands in PluginHive inbox (2026-05-18)
- Angry message from a package recipient: their priority delivery hadn't arrived ("is y'all driver a thief…") (Comment #1)
- Support tried to locate the originating store/merchant from the email and could not — initial inclination was to move the ticket to pending; Keerthi and Vimal flagged as aware (Comment #2)

#### Phase 2: Internal pivot — keep ticket open, fix the root cause (2026-05-18)
- Internal direction (Comment #3): **do not send merchant customer-facing emails from PluginHive email addresses**. If merchants haven't configured SMTP, the app must not send the email at all. Existing merchants need to be migrated to configure and use their own SMTP settings.
- Follow-up: discussed with Veena; confirmed to be included in the upcoming release; moved to `dev_needed` (Comment #4)

### Open Issues (focus of this card: #1)

1. **Block outbound merchant-recipient emails when merchant SMTP is not configured** — Today, when a merchant has no SMTP set up, PluginHive falls back to sending tracking-notification emails from PluginHive addresses. This routes end-customer replies into PluginHive's support queue (as seen with this ticket), exposes PluginHive's domain reputation, and makes the merchant invisible to their own customer. The app must NOT send such emails until SMTP is configured. Blocked: engineering (dev_needed; targeted for upcoming release). Severity: P1 — deliverability/brand/process risk; an unidentifiable end-customer complaint already proves the exposure. Area: `tracking`. (Comments #3, #4)

(Issue #2 — migration of existing merchants to own SMTP — is tracked as ZI-553. Issue #3 — triage path for end-customer emails landing at PluginHive — is tracked as ZI-554.)

## User Story

As a **PluginHive engineering owner who has just had to read an angry end-customer email accusing "y'all driver" of stealing their package — because MCSL's tracking notification went out from a PluginHive address instead of the merchant's**,
I want **MCSL to refuse to send any customer-facing tracking email on behalf of a merchant who has not configured their own SMTP — no silent fallback to a PluginHive sender — and instead surface a clear in-app warning to that merchant ("your tracking notifications are not being sent — configure SMTP")**,
so that **PluginHive's domain reputation stops absorbing every merchant's deliverability headache, end-customers always see the store they actually ordered from in their inbox (not a generic PluginHive address), and an angry parcel-recipient never again lands in our support queue with no merchant we can route them to**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] When a merchant on MCSL has not configured SMTP and a tracking-notification event fires (label generated, shipment in-transit, delivered), MCSL does NOT silently send an email from a PluginHive address — the email is held back and the merchant is notified of the held send
- [ ] The merchant sees a persistent in-app warning on the tracking settings / notifications page reading "SMTP not configured — tracking emails to your customers are not being sent" with a one-click path to the SMTP configuration screen
- [ ] An audit log records every suppressed send (merchant, order, intended recipient, event type, timestamp) so we can prove later that nothing went out under PluginHive's name and so merchants can see what their customers missed
- [ ] After a merchant configures SMTP successfully (verified by a test send) the held queue does NOT auto-flush; merchant explicitly chooses whether to backfill missed notifications or skip — to avoid sending "your package shipped 4 days ago" emails inappropriately
- [ ] Zero tracking emails from MCSL leave PluginHive's domain post-release — verified by a 7-day mail server log audit showing 0 outbound notifications from any pluginhive.com address to end-customer recipients on behalf of merchants who haven't configured SMTP

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Merchant without SMTP triggers a tracking event — nothing leaks
- **Given** an MCSL merchant has not configured SMTP in their app settings
- **When** their shipment hits a tracking milestone (label generated / in-transit / delivered) that would normally trigger an end-customer email
- **Then** no email is sent from any PluginHive domain, the event is recorded in the merchant's audit log as "suppressed: SMTP not configured", and the merchant's tracking-settings page surfaces a clearly named warning with a "Configure SMTP now" call-to-action

### Scenario: Merchant configures SMTP and chooses whether to backfill
- **Given** a merchant who had pending suppressed tracking events for a week and just successfully configured SMTP (verified with a working test send to themselves)
- **When** they open the suppressed-events queue
- **Then** they can preview each suppressed event, choose to skip it (because the package is already delivered and a "shipped" email now would be confusing) or send it (if still relevant), and the chosen sends go out from their newly-configured SMTP — not from PluginHive

### Scenario: Audit proves zero PluginHive-sender tracking emails went out post-release
- **Given** the feature has been live in production for 7 days
- **When** the engineering team queries the mail server logs for outbound tracking notifications from any `*.pluginhive.com` address to end-customer recipients on behalf of MCSL merchants
- **Then** the query returns zero rows — the only outbound emails on MCSL's notification pipeline are from merchant-configured SMTP servers, and the suppressed-event count in the audit log accounts for every event that would have generated a fallback send under the old behaviour
