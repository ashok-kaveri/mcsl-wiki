## User Story

As a **Shopify merchant who frequently searches, filters, and edits products across a large catalogue with hundreds of SKUs**,
I want **the Products page to preserve my current search query, filters, and scroll position when I navigate to a product detail page and then return**,
so that **I do not lose my place in the catalogue and waste time re-entering the same search criteria across every editing session during my 2-3 daily fulfilment batches**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Navigating from a filtered/searched Products list to a product detail page and pressing the browser back button restores the previous search query and filter selections
- [ ] The scroll position within the Products list is restored to where the merchant left off after returning from a product detail page
- [ ] Returning from a product detail page to a paginated Products list restores the same page number the merchant was viewing before navigation
- [ ] Refreshing the Products page itself resets state to the default unfiltered view (preservation only applies to back-navigation)
- [ ] State preservation functions correctly in both Chrome and Safari browsers on desktop

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Search state preserved on back navigation
- **Given** a merchant has typed "welding cable" into the Products page search bar and the filtered results are displayed
- **When** they click on a product to view its details and then press the browser back button
- **Then** the Products page loads with "welding cable" still in the search bar and the same filtered results displayed

### Scenario: Combined filters and pagination preserved
- **Given** a merchant has applied a category filter, sorted by price descending, and navigated to page 3 of the results
- **When** they open a product from page 3, edit its dimensions, save, and navigate back
- **Then** the Products page restores the category filter, price descending sort, and displays page 3 of the results

### Scenario: Full page refresh resets to default state
- **Given** a merchant is viewing a filtered and searched set of products on the Products page
- **When** they press F5 or click the browser refresh button on the Products page itself
- **Then** the page reloads with the default unfiltered, unsorted view showing all products from page 1
