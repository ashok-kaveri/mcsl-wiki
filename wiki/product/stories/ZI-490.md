## User Story

As a **store owner using ShipStation for fulfillment who relies on accurate delivery estimates for customer communication**,
I want **the ability to add buffer days to USPS delivery time estimates shown at checkout to account for my carrier's early pickup schedule**,
so that **customers see realistic delivery dates that account for my fulfillment cutoff times, I don't have to manually explain delays after purchase, and I avoid missed-delivery complaints when USPS picks up earlier than the quoted estimate assumes**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Store owner can configure USPS transit time buffer (e.g., +1 day) in carrier settings
- [ ] Buffer is applied to USPS estimated delivery dates displayed at checkout
- [ ] Buffer applies to all USPS services (Priority Mail, Ground Advantage, etc.)
- [ ] Configuration is saved per carrier and persists across rate requests
- [ ] Bull City Flavors store (#377113) can set +1 day USPS buffer and verify accurate delivery dates at checkout

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Store owner configures USPS transit time buffer
- **Given** Bull City Flavors uses USPS for shipping and needs +1 day buffer for early pickups
- **When** store owner navigates to USPS carrier settings and sets "Transit Time Buffer" to "+1 day"
- **Then** the setting is saved and applied to all future USPS rate quotes
- **And** checkout displays delivery dates that are 1 day later than carrier-returned estimates

### Scenario: Buffer applies to all USPS services
- **Given** store has USPS buffer set to +1 day
- **When** customer adds items to cart and views checkout with USPS Priority Mail, Ground Advantage, and First Class options
- **Then** all USPS service delivery dates reflect the +1 day buffer
- **And** non-USPS carriers (FedEx, UPS) are not affected by the buffer

### Scenario: Customer communication improvement
- **Given** store owner previously had to manually communicate delivery delays due to early pickup
- **When** USPS buffer is configured and checkout rates are displayed
- **Then** customers see realistic delivery dates that account for fulfillment cutoff times
- **And** store owner no longer receives complaints about missed delivery estimates
