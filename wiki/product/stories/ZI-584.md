## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: Recurring Rate Failure Escalation (2026-06-01)
- Customer Danielle Robertson re-opened the issue, referencing a prior ticket (384139) and prior agent reply from May 8
- Her end-client (sieves.net.au / Dave Dick) had customers unable to complete checkout due to no shipping options appearing
- Support confirmed TNT Australia rates were returning at time of investigation (Comment #3)

- Also communicated that a more permanent fix (automatic advancement to next available pickup slot) is in progress (Comment #9)

### Phase 3: Customer Confirmation and Pending Final Fix (2026-06-17 to 2026-06-18)
- Customer confirmed fallback rates are configured and working — no checkout failures since (Comment #11)
- Customer flagged that fallback rates are often inaccurate for their variable-size products, treating this as a temporary workaround (Comment #11)
- Engineering set internal ETA for permanent fix: on or before July 7, 2026 (Comment #15)

## User Story

As a **web developer managing an industrial equipment supplier's WooCommerce store where TNT Australia is the only viable carrier for heavy, variable-weight products**,
I want **the app to automatically request rates from the next available pickup slot when today's TNT pickup window is closed or full**,
so that **my client's B2B customers (universities, labs) always see accurate live shipping costs at checkout instead of flat-rate guesses that either overcharge and lose the sale or undercharge and eat into margins**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] When TNT Australia's API returns a pickup-slot-capacity or cutoff-time error, the app automatically advances the collection date to the next available pickup slot and re-requests live rates
- [ ] The customer's checkout at sieves.net.au displays accurate TNT live rates even after the daily pickup cutoff has passed
- [ ] Fallback rates remain as a last-resort safety net: they are only shown if the automatic slot advancement also fails to retrieve live rates
- [ ] The automatic advancement logic handles weekends, public holidays, and consecutive fully-booked days by scanning forward until a valid slot is found
- [ ] No merchant configuration is required for the automatic advancement — it works out of the box for all TNT Australia service types

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Automatic advancement after daily pickup cutoff
- **Given** a WooCommerce store with TNT Australia configured and the current time is past today's pickup cutoff (e.g., 3:00 PM AEST)
- **When** a customer requests shipping rates at checkout
- **Then** the app automatically advances the collection request to the next business day's first available pickup slot and returns live TNT rates for that date

### Scenario: Consecutive fully-booked days
- **Given** TNT Australia's API reports pickup slots are fully booked for today and the next business day
- **When** a customer requests shipping rates at checkout
- **Then** the app scans forward day-by-day until it finds an available pickup slot, returns live rates for that date, and does not fall back to flat rates unless no slot is available within a configurable horizon (default: 5 business days)

### Scenario: Fallback rate as last resort only
- **Given** TNT Australia's API cannot provide live rates even after advancing through all pickup slots within the scan horizon
- **When** a customer requests shipping rates at checkout
- **Then** the previously configured fallback rate is displayed, and the merchant receives a notification that live rate retrieval failed for an extended period
