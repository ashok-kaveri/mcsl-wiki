## User Story

As an Australian Shopify merchant on the $29 Popular plan with a directly-connected Australia Post eParcel contract, I want my contracted AU Post services to keep returning rates at checkout when Australia Post updates their service codes upstream, so that I'm not silently losing sales for days while my checkout hides AU Post and shows only my more expensive Fastway option — and I don't have to discover the outage myself, chase support, and threaten to leave just to get the same code-map fix that's already shipped in your standalone AU Post app applied to the MCSL product I'm actually paying for.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] TheTechDen.com.au's checkout returns AU Post eParcel rates for the same heavy-item cart (SKU `HYDEDGE44HD`) where it currently shows only Fastway, without the merchant changing any configuration.
- [ ] The AU Post eParcel service-code mapping that exists in the standalone AU Post app is ported into MCSL so that all MCSL merchants with an eParcel account get rates from the same carriers as before the code update — not just TheTechDen.
- [ ] When AU Post (or any carrier MCSL integrates with) publishes service-code changes in future, the change is detected and tracked centrally (single ticket / single mapping update) so it cannot ship to one PluginHive product and silently break another.
- [ ] A merchant-facing rate-log signal exists for "rate returned 0 services because no service codes matched" so support (and customers) can identify a code-mapping outage in the first reply instead of after a multi-day investigation.
- [ ] Regression test covers AU Post eParcel rate response for a representative heavy-item Australian cart so this specific failure mode cannot regress unnoticed.

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: TheTechDen's checkout returns AU Post rates again (happy path for the reporting customer)
- **Given** TheTechDen.com.au has the MCSL app installed on the $29 Popular plan
- **And** their Australia Post eParcel (MPB) carrier is connected with the same credentials they had on 2026-06-01
- **And** a customer adds SKU `HYDEDGE44HD` to the cart and proceeds to checkout with an Australian shipping address
- **When** Shopify requests rates from MCSL
- **Then** the rate response includes at least one AU Post eParcel service alongside Aramex Fastway
- **And** the rate-log entry shows the matched AU Post service codes (not an empty mapping)

### Scenario: The fix covers every MCSL merchant on AU Post eParcel — not only TheTechDen
- **Given** a different MCSL merchant has an AU Post eParcel account connected with a different set of contracted services
- **And** Australia Post has rolled out the new service codes upstream
- **When** that merchant's checkout fires a rate request through MCSL
- **Then** rates are returned for every eParcel service the merchant is contracted for under the new codes
- **And** support does not have to apply a per-merchant patch — the same service-code mapping in MCSL serves all eParcel accounts (parity with the standalone AU Post app)

### Scenario: The next carrier service-code change is detected, not silently broken
- **Given** a carrier MCSL integrates with publishes a service-code change (test simulation: a service code in the AU Post mapping is renamed or removed)
- **When** the next rate request runs against that carrier
- **Then** the rate engine logs a structured signal that a known service code is no longer in the carrier response (e.g. `unmatched_carrier_service_code=<code>`)
- **And** that signal surfaces in the support-facing rate log AND is alertable, so the outage is opened as a single internal ticket within hours rather than being discovered by an individual merchant several days later
