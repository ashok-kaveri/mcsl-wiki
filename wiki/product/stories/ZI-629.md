## User Story

As a **heritage food manufacturer on a Yearly Popular plan whose "Create Your Own" gift boxes drive gifting-season revenue and whose external dev team has already ruled out variable-product workarounds**,
I want **WSS to recognize and import WooCommerce Composite Products so that orders containing multi-flavour gift boxes flow through the same label-generation pipeline as simple products**,
so that **I stop manually processing gift-box shipments outside the plugin — a workaround that has cost my small team 9 months of wasted hours, delayed holiday fulfillment prep, and forced me to hold off investing dev budget in my store because the shipping integration might eventually catch up**.

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Orders containing WooCommerce Composite Products (SomewhereWarm plugin) are imported into WSS and appear in the order grid
- [ ] Composite product line items display their component items (e.g., individual jam flavours) with correct quantities and weights for accurate rate calculation
- [ ] Label generation succeeds for orders with composite products using UPS, Canpar, and Canada Post — the three carriers Greaves Jams uses
- [ ] The specific product "3 x 250ml Citrine & Navy Gift Box — Create Your Own" that remains stuck is imported after the fix
- [ ] Existing simple and variable product imports are unaffected (no regression)
- [ ] Product import handles the hidden-product pattern (`2500-CUSTOM` SKU linked to composite) without creating duplicate or orphaned product records

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Composite product order imports successfully
- **Given** a WooCommerce store with the SomewhereWarm Composite Products plugin installed and a composite product "6 x 250ml Canadian Tradition Gift Box — Create Your Own" with 6 selected jam flavours
- **When** a customer places an order containing that composite product and WSS runs its order import
- **Then** the order appears in the WSS order grid with the composite line item and its component items listed, and the total weight reflects the sum of all component weights

### Scenario: Label generation works for composite product orders across all carrier types
- **Given** an imported order containing a composite product with correct weights and dimensions
- **When** the merchant selects the order and generates a shipping label via UPS, Canpar, or Canada Post
- **Then** the label is generated successfully with accurate package weight derived from the composite product's components

### Scenario: Mixed orders with simple, variable, and composite products
- **Given** an order containing one simple product, one variable product, and one composite product
- **When** WSS imports the order
- **Then** all three line items are imported correctly, each with their respective weights, and no product type causes the import to skip or fail silently

### Scenario: Previously self-resolved products remain stable after code change
- **Given** Greaves Jams' 4 composite products that self-imported without a fix (2×250ml Deluxe, 4×250ml Premium, 6×250ml Canadian Tradition, 12×250ml Connoisseur)
- **When** the composite product support code is deployed
- **Then** these 4 products continue to import correctly and no duplicate or broken product records are created
