## User Story

As an **Indian D2C Shopify merchant (Aswini Homeo) who's losing margin to Amazon Shipping return rates in low-deliverability pincodes**,
I want **MCSL to call Amazon's Verified Address API before I commit a label, and skip Amazon Shipping for orders Amazon itself flags as poorly serviceable**,
so that **I can confidently route my full domestic volume through PluginHive at scale without watching returns eat the savings, and Amazon's sales team has a concrete reason to keep recommending MCSL to other India merchants instead of standing up an in-house tool**.

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] Aswini Homeo can enable the Amazon VA check on the Amazon Shipping India carrier connector in MCSL settings, with the toggle defaulted to "off" for existing carrier connections so no merchant's flow changes silently
- [ ] When VA is enabled and the order's destination is flagged "not serviceable" by Amazon, MCSL marks the order with a clearly named status (e.g. "Amazon: address unverified") and excludes it from auto-generate-label batches for Amazon Shipping
- [ ] The VA call adds no more than 800ms median to per-order label generation latency on Aswini's catalogue (measured across 100 sequential orders), and a single VA failure does not block label generation when an alternative carrier is selected
- [ ] An automation-rule condition "Amazon VA verdict" (verified / unverified / unknown) is available so Aswini can route unverified orders to Delhivery/XpressBees instead of Amazon, without manual intervention
- [ ] After rollout, Aswini's Amazon Shipping return rate on filtered batches is measurable and reportable in MCSL's standard order export within 30 days of go-live (so we can prove the ROI to Amazon Shipping India sales)

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Aswini Homeo routes only Amazon-verified addresses through Amazon Shipping
- **Given** Aswini Homeo has enabled the Amazon VA toggle on their Amazon Shipping India carrier and configured an automation rule that routes "Amazon VA: unverified" orders to Delhivery
- **When** a batch of 100 fresh Shopify orders is auto-processed for label generation
- **Then** each order is annotated with Amazon's VA verdict in MCSL's order grid, orders flagged "unverified" are routed to Delhivery by the automation rule, and orders flagged "verified" generate Amazon Shipping labels in the same batch with no extra clicks

### Scenario: Other Indian merchants on Amazon Shipping can enable VA without engineering involvement
- **Given** any Shopify merchant on the Amazon Shipping India carrier (not just Aswini) opens the carrier settings page after the MCSL 381 release
- **When** they look at the Amazon Shipping connector options
- **Then** the VA toggle is visible with inline help explaining what it does (filters orders to deliverable addresses) and what it costs (one API call per rate/label request), and enabling it requires zero engineering or support intervention

### Scenario: VA API outage or rate-limit does not block all Amazon labels
- **Given** Amazon's VA endpoint is returning 5xx errors or rate-limit responses for a sustained 5-minute window
- **When** Aswini Homeo tries to generate Amazon labels during that window with VA enabled
- **Then** MCSL falls back to "Amazon VA: unknown" verdict, surfaces a banner in the carrier connector page noting the upstream issue, and continues to allow label generation for merchants who explicitly route unknown-verdict orders through Amazon — instead of failing every Amazon label silently
