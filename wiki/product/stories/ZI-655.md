## User Story

As a **Shopify merchant who adds packaging material costs to shipping rates and needs to compare what the customer paid against live carrier quotes**,
I want **the customer-selected shipping service name and the price they paid to both appear in the MCSL order summary page**,
so that **I can quickly decide which carrier service to use for each order without switching back to Shopify admin to look up the customer's shipping charge**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] The order summary page displays the customer-selected shipping service name and the amount the customer paid (from Shopify's shipping_lines data)
- [ ] The customer-paid rate is shown alongside or near the carrier rate quotes so the merchant can visually compare them
- [ ] Orders with multiple shipping lines (e.g., split shipments) display each line's service name and amount
- [ ] Orders with no shipping line data (e.g., local pickup, free shipping) display an appropriate indicator rather than blank or zero
- [ ] The displayed amount uses the store's currency and formatting consistent with the rest of the order summary

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Customer-paid shipping rate visible in order summary
- **Given** a Shopify order where the customer selected "Standard Shipping" at checkout and paid AUD 12.50 for it
- **When** the merchant opens the order summary page in MCSL to generate a label
- **Then** the order summary displays "Standard Shipping — AUD 12.50" alongside the live carrier rate quotes, enabling comparison

### Scenario: Free shipping orders show zero-cost indicator
- **Given** a Shopify order where the customer used a free shipping discount code and paid AUD 0.00 for shipping
- **When** the merchant opens the order summary page in MCSL
- **Then** the shipping line displays the service name with "AUD 0.00" or "Free Shipping" rather than leaving the field blank

### Scenario: Orders imported before enhancement retain correct display
- **Given** an order that was imported into MCSL before this enhancement was deployed, with shipping_lines data already stored
- **When** the merchant opens that order's summary page after the update
- **Then** the customer-paid rate is displayed using the existing shipping_lines data from the Shopify import, without requiring a re-import
