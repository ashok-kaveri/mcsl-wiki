## User Story

As a **Shopify merchant onboarded through the Amazon Shipping partnership, printing 89+ Amazon labels a month and needing to reconcile which ones actually reached customers**,
I want **Amazon Shipping tracking statuses — including the final Delivered event — to flow back into the app the same way they do for every other carrier**,
so that **I can close out fulfilled orders confidently, trigger post-delivery workflows (reviews, restocks, support follow-up), and stop manually cross-checking the Amazon portal to answer "where is my package?" questions from customers**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] KS International's Amazon Shipping orders transition from "Fulfilled" to "In Transit" to "Delivered" in MCSL based on Amazon's tracking feed, for shipments already in flight
- [ ] The Amazon Shipping tracking-sync pipeline is confirmed to poll/receive status updates on the same cadence as other carriers (e.g., matching the cadence of USPS/FedEx tracking)
- [ ] Amazon Shipping delivered events trigger the same downstream workflows (webhooks, Shopify fulfillment status updates) as other carriers
- [ ] A monitoring alert fires if Amazon tracking-sync lag exceeds a threshold (e.g., zero delivered events in N hours across all merchants), preventing silent regressions
- [ ] The Amazon Shipping integration page shows a "last tracking sync" timestamp so merchants can self-diagnose if something has stopped

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Restore Amazon tracking for KS International
- **Given** KS International has Amazon Shipping labels generated in the past 30 days with tracking numbers
- **When** the tracking-sync pipeline runs after the fix
- **Then** every shipment's current Amazon status is fetched and reflected in MCSL
- **And** shipments actually delivered show "Delivered" with timestamp, not stuck on "Fulfilled"

### Scenario: Validate ongoing parity with other carriers
- **Given** a merchant has shipments across Amazon, USPS, FedEx, and DHL on the same day
- **When** 24 hours later each shipment has been delivered per carrier data
- **Then** all four carriers show the same Delivered state in MCSL
- **And** the status-update latency for Amazon is comparable to other carriers (within the same polling window)

### Scenario: Detect Amazon tracking regressions before customers do
- **Given** Amazon tracking-sync pipeline health is being monitored
- **When** the rate of new delivered-events drops to zero for N hours across all Amazon shipments
- **Then** an internal alert is fired to the tracking team
- **And** the Amazon integration page shows a degraded-sync banner to affected merchants until resolved
