## User Story

As a **$49 Premium-plan Shopify merchant running a 2,290-order art-print store where the app's sole job is to show accurate real-time shipping rates at checkout**,
I want **any weight change I make on a product in Shopify to be reflected in MCSL within minutes — without having to open a support ticket or trigger a manual force-import**,
so that **my checkout doesn't quote inflated rates that scare customers away from completing purchases, and I don't lose conversions every day the stale weight persists on live SKUs**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] SKUs 8BIT-8133-114, -115, -116 on iam8bit.myshopify.com show 0.3 oz in MCSL matching Shopify, and the checkout rate reflects the correct weight
- [ ] Editing a product weight in Shopify (both when unit is oz and when unit is lb) propagates to MCSL within 15 minutes via webhook/sync, for both existing and new products
- [ ] Force-import / manual re-sync of products actually overwrites the cached weight value (not just touches metadata) — verified by re-running it on a product with a known stale weight and seeing the updated value
- [ ] Unit-conversion between oz and lb is covered by automated tests (0.3 oz ≠ 0.99 lb; 16 oz = 1 lb), preventing regression of the conversion defect
- [ ] Customers on Premium plans see a "weight last synced" timestamp on each product in MCSL so they can self-diagnose sync lag without filing a ticket

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Fix the three stuck SKUs for iam8bit
- **Given** iam8bit.myshopify.com has products 8BIT-8133-114/115/116 showing 0.99 lb in MCSL while Shopify shows 0.3 oz
- **When** the L3 team runs the product-sync pipeline (or the fixed force-import flow) on those SKUs
- **Then** MCSL reflects 0.3 oz for all three products
- **And** a rate request at checkout for these products returns the small-parcel rate (not the 15-oz rate)

### Scenario: Generalize the fix across all products and all merchants
- **Given** any Shopify product exists in MCSL with a previously cached weight
- **When** the merchant updates the weight in Shopify (any unit, any direction, new or existing product)
- **Then** MCSL persists the new weight within 15 minutes
- **And** a QA run exercises oz↔lb↔g↔kg round-trips to confirm no unit-conversion drift

### Scenario: Prevent silent sync failures from recurring
- **Given** a product-weight sync fails or is skipped for any reason (API error, webhook miss, cache collision)
- **When** the merchant views the product in MCSL
- **Then** a "weight last synced" timestamp is visible, and if older than 1 hour, a visual indicator flags the product
- **And** the merchant can trigger a targeted re-sync for just that product without contacting support
