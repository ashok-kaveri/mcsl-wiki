## User Story

As a **small NZ-based Shopify merchant shipping less than NZD 10k of NZ Post a year — exactly the segment NZ Post itself stood up MyNZPB for because we don't qualify for their deferred-payment business credit accounts**,
I want **to connect my MyNZPB (My NZ Post for Business) account directly to MCSL, fetch rates against my MyNZPB pricing, and generate MyNZPB-compliant labels from MCSL just like a larger merchant connects a credit-account NZ Post connector today**,
so that **I can stop printing NZ Post labels manually on the MyNZPB portal one-by-one (or worse, picking another carrier MCSL supports), get the same automation rules and rate-shopping that bigger merchants get, and PluginHive can grow into the SMB segment NZ Post has explicitly opened up — analogous to what AusPost MPB unlocked for Aussie SMBs**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] An NZ-based merchant with a MyNZPB account (and no deferred-payment NZ Post credit account) can connect their MyNZPB credentials inside MCSL as a distinct carrier connector — separate UI from the existing credit-account NZ Post connector so the merchant knows which they are setting up
- [ ] Once connected, the merchant can fetch live MyNZPB rates for a Shopify order, generate a MyNZPB-compliant label (per NZ Post's MyNZPB label spec), and the resulting tracking number writes back into the order grid like any other carrier
- [ ] MCSL surfaces MyNZPB-specific pricing/billing nuances in the connector help text (e.g. that MyNZPB charges per shipment vs deferred-payment terms) so merchants understand which connector to pick
- [ ] Automation rules can branch on "carrier: MyNZPB" so a merchant who graduates from MyNZPB to a credit account later can switch connector type without rebuilding their rule set from scratch
- [ ] The integration is dark-launched behind a feature flag, then enabled for a pilot of 5–10 MyNZPB-eligible merchants for one sprint before full release — verifies real-world NZ Post billing reconciliation before broad rollout

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: An SMB Shopify merchant connects MyNZPB and ships their first label end-to-end
- **Given** an NZ-based Shopify merchant who has a MyNZPB account but does not qualify for an NZ Post deferred-payment credit account
- **When** they open MCSL's "Add Carrier" list and select MyNZPB
- **Then** the connection flow specifically requests MyNZPB credentials (not the credit-account flow), connects successfully, and they can generate a MyNZPB-compliant label for a real Shopify order within 15 minutes of starting setup — without needing PluginHive support to flip a backend toggle

### Scenario: Rate-shopping side-by-side with other MCSL carriers works for MyNZPB
- **Given** a small merchant with both MyNZPB and (say) FedEx connected in MCSL
- **When** they trigger a rate request on a domestic NZ order
- **Then** both MyNZPB and FedEx rates appear in the rate panel side-by-side, the merchant can pick MyNZPB or apply an automation rule that routes orders under a weight threshold to MyNZPB, and the chosen carrier produces a working label and tracking number

### Scenario: Graduation path from MyNZPB to NZ Post credit account preserves merchant workflow
- **Given** a merchant who started on MyNZPB, used MCSL for 9 months, built automation rules referencing the MyNZPB connector, and has now qualified for a deferred-payment NZ Post account
- **When** they add the credit-account NZ Post connector alongside MyNZPB and update their automation rules to point at the new connector
- **Then** the rules continue to work without manual recreation, MyNZPB stays available (so they can keep using it for low-value shipments if they choose), and there is no "carrier conflict" error from MCSL when both connectors are live on the same account
