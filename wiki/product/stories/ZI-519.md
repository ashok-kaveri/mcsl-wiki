## User Story

As a **Shopify merchant who ships regulated products (lithium batteries, fragrances, cosmetics with alcohol, cleaning chemicals, e-bike kits) on a FedEx account that has been migrated to REST-only credentials**,
I want **FedEx hazmat / dangerous-goods shipments to be generable from inside MCSL on REST exactly as they were on SOAP — including the IATA/IMDG declaration, UN ID, packing group, and Shipper's Declaration paperwork**,
so that **I don't lose the ability to ship my regulated catalogue the day FedEx forces me onto REST, I'm not driven back to FedEx Ship Manager and manual paperwork for every battery shipment, and PluginHive's REST migration story doesn't quietly drop one of the most compliance-sensitive use-cases in our customer base**.

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Simple)

- [ ] DG/hazmat-marked Shopify orders can be processed through MCSL on a FedEx REST account end-to-end (rate → label → manifest → tracking) without falling back to SOAP or FedEx Ship Manager
- [ ] The plugin's product-level / order-level DG configuration (UN ID, proper shipping name, hazard class, packing group, quantity per package, accessible/inaccessible) is sent on the FedEx REST `shipdghazmat/v1` request payload
- [ ] FedEx returns the required hazmat documents (Shipper's Declaration, OP-900 / 1421-C as applicable) and the plugin surfaces them to the merchant alongside the shipping label
- [ ] Validation errors from FedEx's hazmat endpoint (missing UN ID, unsupported lane, expired training certification, non-DG-approved account) surface as actionable messages — not raw REST payloads
- [ ] An internal QA matrix covers at least one shipment per major hazard class (Class 3 flammable liquid, Class 9 lithium battery section II, ORM-D) on the FedEx REST sandbox before release

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Given/When/Then)

### Scenario: Generating a hazmat label on FedEx REST works at SOAP parity
- **Given** a Shopify MCSL merchant on a FedEx REST account has a product flagged as Class 9 lithium-ion battery (UN3480, section II) and an order containing it
- **When** they click "Generate Label" on the order grid
- **Then** the plugin calls FedEx's `shipdghazmat/v1` endpoint with the full DG payload (UN ID, proper shipping name, packing group, quantity, accessible/inaccessible flag) and receives a label plus Shipper's Declaration
- **And** the order is marked `labelled` in Shopify and the DG paperwork is attached / downloadable in the same flow as the label

### Scenario: REST migration doesn't silently drop hazmat capability
- **Given** an existing MCSL customer was shipping DG on FedEx SOAP and is forced to migrate to REST (per FedEx's API sunset)
- **When** they re-authenticate with REST credentials and reload their carrier profile
- **Then** every DG-marked product / preset that worked on SOAP continues to generate labels on REST without manual reconfiguration
- **And** the plugin's REST-migration checklist explicitly confirms "Dangerous goods supported: Yes" so merchants don't discover the gap mid-shipment

### Scenario: Compliance and safety errors are caught before the label prints
- **Given** a merchant configures a product as DG but is missing a required field (e.g. no UN ID, or training expired on their FedEx account, or the destination doesn't accept that hazard class)
- **When** the plugin attempts to generate the label
- **Then** the merchant sees a human-readable error pointing to the exact missing/invalid field, with a link to the relevant FedEx hazmat documentation
- **And** no label is generated and no Shopify fulfillment is created — protecting the merchant from shipping a non-compliant package and the regulatory exposure that follows
