# Ticket #384139 — TNT Australia Rates Fail After Pickup Cutoff Time; Automatic Next-Day Slot Selection Not Implemented

- **Customer**: Sahas Pokhrel, Project Manager at WP Creative (csid.com.au)
- **Product**: Shopify Multi Carrier Shipping Label
- **Duration**: 2026-04-15 to 2026-06-18 (65 days, ongoing)

## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: Initial Rate Failure — Incorrect Pickup Time (2026-04-15)
- Customer reported rates failing at checkout after store closing time, blocking 24/7 order placement.
- Support identified that the TNT Australia pickup time in plugin settings had been set to 2 PM, which exceeded the regional depot cutoff.
- Support manually corrected it to 1 PM; rates resumed. Customer asked not to CC their client (ezabelle@csid.com.au) on tickets.

### Phase 2: Recurring Failure — Same Root Cause Resurfaces (2026-04-17)
- Rates failed again despite pickup time remaining at 1 PM. Support toggled the setting between 1 PM, 2 PM, 3 PM — 2 PM and 3 PM still failed; 1 PM worked again.
- Customer escalated urgency, requested JSON responses and direct developer access.
- Internal Slack thread opened (C02E15X8X0C). Technical explanation shared: plugin passes current-day date before cutoff, and next working day after cutoff. When TNT blocks slots due to capacity constraints, requests with any time fail intermittently.
- Engineering acknowledged the issue; ticket tagged dev_needed.

### Phase 3: Fallback Rate Feature Deployed (2026-05-26 to 2026-06-04)
- Engineering introduced a Fallback Rate option at the service level for TNT Australia, allowing a fixed rate to display when live rates cannot be fetched.
- Support communicated to customer (Comment #24): fallback rates are configurable under Plugin Settings > Shipping Rates > Carrier Services > TNT Australia > Enable Fallback Rates.
- Engineering confirmed automatic next-available-slot scheduling is still in development (ETA on or before 2026-07-07, per Comment #25).
- A second customer reported the same issue (Ticket #393054), confirming this is not isolated.

## Open Issues

1. **Automatic Next-Day/Next-Slot Advancement for TNT Australia Rates Not Yet Shipped** — When TNT Australia blocks collection slots due to operational capacity, the plugin does not automatically advance the request to the next available pickup day or slot. The fallback rate workaround is live but requires manual configuration and does not display live rates. The permanent fix (auto-advance scheduling) is in progress with ETA 2026-07-07. Blocked: customer waiting on feature delivery; engineering has the item scoped. Severity: recurring checkout disruption for an active merchant; second customer confirmed affected (Ticket #393054). Area: rate-shopping. (Comment #24, Comment #25)

2. **Client Email Incorrectly CC'd on Support Thread** — Customer requested multiple times that their client ezabelle@csid.com.au not be included in ticket correspondence. The client also sent a removal request directly. Support acknowledged and removed the address, but the ticket remains open with no confirmation the CRM/CC list was permanently corrected for future tickets. Blocked: customer/support — trust risk if client is CC'd again on subsequent tickets. Severity: low-severity but reputational. Area: other. (Comment #18, Comment #19, Comment #20)

## Resolved Issues

1. **TNT Australia Rates Failing Due to Pickup Time Set to 2 PM** — Support corrected the plugin pickup time from 2 PM to 1 PM matching the regional depot cutoff. Rates resumed. (Comment #4)

2. **Fallback Rate Option Deployed for 24/7 Checkout Continuity** — Engineering introduced a configurable fallback rate for TNT Australia services so customers can complete purchases even when live rates are unavailable due to TNT slot blocking. Setup instructions communicated to merchant. (Comment #24)
