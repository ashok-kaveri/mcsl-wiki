# Ticket #390467 — End-customer complaint via PluginHive email: SMTP-config gap exposed

- **Customer**: Unidentified end-recipient (their package late) — store could not be located by support; the ticket was triggered because a merchant tracking-notification email was sent FROM a PluginHive email address, so the recipient's angry reply landed in PluginHive's queue rather than the merchant's
- **Product**: Tag is `shopify` but no specific app tag; lives in `other_platforms`; product-scope item affecting any PluginHive Shopify/notification app where merchants haven't configured their own SMTP
- **Duration**: Single-day filing (2026-05-18)

## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: End-customer complaint lands in PluginHive inbox (2026-05-18)
- Angry message from a package recipient: their priority delivery hadn't arrived ("is y'all driver a thief…") (Comment #1)
- Support tried to locate the originating store/merchant from the email and could not — initial inclination was to move the ticket to pending; Keerthi and Vimal flagged as aware (Comment #2)

### Phase 2: Internal pivot — keep ticket open, fix the root cause (2026-05-18)
- Internal direction (Comment #3): **do not send merchant customer-facing emails from PluginHive email addresses**. If merchants haven't configured SMTP, the app must not send the email at all. Existing merchants need to be migrated to configure and use their own SMTP settings.
- Follow-up: discussed with Veena; confirmed to be included in the upcoming release; moved to `dev_needed` (Comment #4)

## Open Issues

1. **Block outbound merchant-recipient emails when merchant SMTP is not configured** — Today, when a merchant has no SMTP set up, PluginHive falls back to sending tracking-notification emails from PluginHive addresses. This routes end-customer replies into PluginHive's support queue (as seen with this ticket), exposes PluginHive's domain reputation, and makes the merchant invisible to their own customer. The app must NOT send such emails until SMTP is configured. Blocked: engineering (dev_needed; targeted for upcoming release). Severity: P1 — deliverability/brand/process risk; an unidentifiable end-customer complaint already proves the exposure. Area: `tracking`. (Comments #3, #4)

2. **Migrate existing merchants to merchant-owned SMTP configuration** — One-time migration / outreach to existing merchants who never configured SMTP, prompting them to set it up (and/or opt out of the auto-notifications). Required to actually drain the population of merchants currently routing through PluginHive addresses. Blocked: product + CS process. Severity: P2 — companion to issue #1; without this the change above will create gaps. Area: `tracking`. (Comment #3)

3. **Triage path when end-customer complaints reach PluginHive's queue** — This ticket exposed that when an end-customer-to-PluginHive email arrives, support has no reliable way to identify the originating merchant. Need either an envelope-tag/Reply-To convention or a lookup hop so support can route to the correct merchant rather than closing as untraceable. Blocked: support process + engineering. Severity: P3. Area: `tracking`. (Comments #1, #2)
