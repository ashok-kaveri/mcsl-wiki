# Ticket #394205 — Royal Mail Rates Card Outdated (2024 vs 2026) Blocking Accurate Checkout Rates

- **Customer**: Luke Mason, Managing Director (keylibrary.co.uk / keylibraryuk.myshopify.com)
- **Product**: Shopify Multi Carrier Shipping Label
- **Duration**: 2026-06-08 to 2026-06-18 (10 days, ongoing)

## Timeline & Key Phases

### Phase 1: DHL Express Label Generation Errors — Three-Way Debug with DHL CFIT (2026-06-08 to 2026-06-11)
- Ticket opened as a forwarded email thread between customer (Key Library), PluginHive, and DHL's Customer Facing IT (CFIT UK) team regarding label generation failures.
- DHL CFIT disputed ownership, claiming they only receive data from PluginHive and do not control label generation. (Comment #1)
- PluginHive confirmed the joint thread was received and stated they would wait for DHL's input before acting. (Comment #2)
- After no DHL response, PluginHive proposed testing label generation on a real test order. Customer provided order KL19470. (Comment #11–#12)
- Label generated successfully for KL19470; DHL API errors from the original complaint were resolved. Customer cancelled the test label to avoid charges. (Comments #13–#15)

### Phase 2: Pre-Shipment Questions — International Customs, Weight Adjustments, Insurance (2026-06-11)
- Customer preparing for first live international shipment (~£600 value) and asked about: weight override for packaging, extra insurance, and where to enter consignee VAT and EORI numbers. (Comments #16–#20)
- PluginHive clarified: weight editable in Packaging Summary; insurance available via declared value toggle; only one Tax ID field available for recipient — both VAT and EORI must share it as DHL API provides a single field. (Comment #21)
- Customer flagged that app allows label creation even when country of origin is not filled in, which DHL requires for customs. PluginHive acknowledged it and provided a video on adding customs info to products. (Comments #23–#26)

### Phase 3: Royal Mail Integration Setup for International Checkout Rates (2026-06-11 to 2026-06-16)
- Customer requested Royal Mail for international/EU only (not UK), using OBA account. (Comment #29)
- PluginHive initially directed customer to EasyPost route. Customer asked about direct Royal Mail integration. (Comments #30–#35)
- PluginHive confirmed: direct Royal Mail API integration is currently on hold in the app (minimum 25,000 shipments/year required); Royal Mail Rates (rate card display, no label generation) is the available option. Customer confirmed 25k+ shipments/year but stated rate card is sufficient since international volume is low. (Comments #37–#39)
- Automation rule setup for showing Royal Mail Rates only for non-UK destinations attempted over multiple email exchanges; rates not showing at checkout after following video instructions. (Comments #41–#47)
- Support escalated to Zoom call; call held 2026-06-16 at 18:30 London time. Support assisted with: Royal Mail Rates account connection, shipping zone setup, automation rule to restrict to international, packaging settings for Royal Mail letter packaging. Customer confirmed rates working at checkout. (Comments #49–#52)

### Phase 4: Outdated Royal Mail Rates Card Discovered — Dev Escalation (2026-06-16 to 2026-06-18)
- During call, customer noticed rates shown at checkout do not match Royal Mail's own rates calculator. Root cause identified: MCSL is using April 2024 Royal Mail rates card; current card is April 2026. (Comment #52)
- L3 escalation raised by Manohar V with links to both the live April 2026 Royal Mail PDF and the current outdated card in MCSL's CDN. (Comment #53)
- L3 (Veena) confirmed enhancement will be built to update the rates card; ticket tagged `dev_needed`. (Comment #54)
- Support informed customer via call; customer asked for ETA. (Comments #57–#59)
- ETA confirmed internally as on or before 7 July 2026; communicated to customer. Customer acknowledged. (Comments #65–#67)

## Open Issues

1. **Royal Mail 2024 Rates Card Still Active in Production** — The MCSL app is serving the April 2024 Royal Mail price list (`cdn.pluginhive.io/third-party/docs/royalmail/our-prices-april-2024-v1.pdf`) instead of the April 2026 version. Rates shown at checkout are incorrect and do not match the Royal Mail public rates calculator. The customer is actively using Royal Mail Rates at checkout for international orders and will see wrong prices until this is fixed. Blocked: engineering (update rates asset and deploy). Severity: confirmed production defect affecting live checkout rates for all Royal Mail Rates users; ETA committed as on or before 7 July 2026. Area: rate-shopping. (Comment #53)

## Resolved Issues

1. **DHL Express Label Generation Failures** — Label generation errors reported via DHL CFIT thread were resolved. Test label for order KL19470 generated successfully. Root cause not documented in thread but confirmed working. (Comment #13)
2. **Recipient EORI Field Missing at Order Level** — Customer needed to pass both VAT and EORI for consignee. Support clarified DHL API only exposes one Tax ID field for recipient; both must be combined in that field. Shipper EORI goes in From Address settings. Customer accepted this limitation. (Comment #21)
3. **Country of Origin Not Enforced on Label Creation** — Customer flagged that the app allows label generation without country of origin, which DHL requires for customs clearance. Support provided a walkthrough video for adding customs info to products. Not a hard enforcement fix but customer was unblocked. (Comments #23–#26)
4. **Royal Mail Rates Automation Rule Not Displaying at Checkout** — After multiple email troubleshooting attempts failed, resolved on the support call (2026-06-16). Support set up the automation rule (Destination Country != United Kingdom) and confirmed rates appearing at checkout for EU/international orders. (Comment #52)
5. **Royal Mail Direct API Integration Route Confusion** — Customer wanted direct OBA integration without EasyPost. Clarified: direct Royal Mail API integration is currently on hold in the app; Royal Mail Rates (rate card) is the available path and meets the customer's actual need (rate display only, no label generation needed for international). (Comments #37–#39)
